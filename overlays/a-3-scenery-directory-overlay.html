这是一个以"大跨空间"为主题的课程设计项目，功能定位为多功能活动中心，选址位于苏州大学独墅湖校区内。考虑到场地处于高新区，具备开放和前瞻性的实验氛围，因此设计上可以更大胆、富有创新性。在多数人将"大跨"理解为横向延展的空间时，我尝试反其道而行之，探索竖向发展的可能性。项目以"瞭望塔"的形式介入校园，希望激发学生向上探索、攀登的能量，营造一个富有活力的立体公共空间。
This is a course project focused on long-span structural design, with the assigned program being a multifunctional center. The site is located within the Dushu Lake campus of Soochow University, in a high-tech development zone that encourages bold and innovative architectural approaches. While most long-span designs tend to emphasize horizontal expansion, I chose to explore the potential of vertical development. The project takes the form of a "watchtower" within the campus, aiming to inspire students to look upward, climb, and explore. It serves as a vibrant and dynamic vertical public space embedded in the academic environment.
