这是我在米兰理工大学研一秋季学期的建筑遗产保护设计课（Architectural Preservation Studio）中的课程项目。整个项目分为三个阶段：在前期阶段，我们利用QGIS对场地的道路、水系、景观以及城市化过程进行历史演变分析，同时梳理了Villa Arconati自身的发展脉络；中期阶段，我们以Villa Arconati的马厩区域为研究对象，通过Metashape构建三维模型进行衰败分析，并进一步研究其当前的结构状态与使用情况；在后期阶段，我们基于前期的调研成果，提出了针对Villa Arconati的整体干预、更新与改造策略。我们小组认为，Villa Arconati不仅具有重要的历史文化价值，同时也拥有珍贵的自然资源。它坐落于一片宁静的森林中，自然环境优越，非常适合发展为一个自然疗愈空间。因此我们提出了"以自然疗愈健康"（Nature for Health）为项目主题。我们首先将整个Villa区域进行分区评估，并根据每一区域的现状提出针对性的更新策略。接着，我们分析了潜在的使用人群，提出了"五感疗愈"的概念，在不同区域中嵌入嗅觉、听觉、视觉、触觉与味觉的体验装置，营造多样化的感官互动空间。最终我们聚焦于马厩的更新设计，将其改造成游客接待中心，并在庭院中延续了巴洛克式花园的几何布局，构建一个浓缩的"五感导览路径"，作为整个Villa疗愈体验的起点和汇聚点。
This is a project from my Architectural Preservation Studio during the first year of my Master's studies at Politecnico di Milano, in the fall semester. The project was carried out in three main phases: In the initial phase, we used QGIS to analyze the historical evolution of the site's roads, water systems, landscape, and urbanization patterns, along with a timeline study of Villa Arconati's development; In the middle phase, we focused on the stable area of Villa Arconati. We built a 3D model using Metashape for decay analysis and conducted in-depth research on its current physical and functional condition; In the final phase, we proposed a comprehensive strategy for intervention, renewal, and adaptive reuse based on our research findings. Our group identified that Villa Arconati not only holds significant historical value but is also endowed with precious natural surroundings. Nestled in a quiet forest, the site offers ideal conditions to be reimagined as a place for nature-based healing. This led us to develop the project under the theme "Nature for Health." We began by conducting a zoning assessment of the entire Villa complex and proposed tailored renewal strategies for each area. After analyzing the needs of potential users, we introduced the concept of "Five Senses Healing," embedding sensory experiences—smell, hearing, sight, touch, and taste—into each zone to create rich and interactive environments. Finally, we concentrated on the transformation of the stable into a visitors' center. In its courtyard, we reinterpreted the geometry of the Baroque garden to create a condensed experiential path based on the five senses, serving as both an introduction and culmination to the healing journey throughout the villa.
